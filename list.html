1. Ameyoko Shopping District
    I entered Ameyoko from Okachimachi Station. Try capturing the "Ameyoko" Sign at this entrance.
  2. Nata Shoten
    You know how I said Ameyoko was a black market after WWII? This is a fun shop with some WWII era items!
  3. Uo Kusa
    A small standing bar with seafood from Tohoku, friendly prices too...
  4. Ameyoko Plaza
    There is a Tokyo Whiskey Store in this plaza, I must have entered from the entrance closest to that. Take a picture of the Plaza Sign at the entrance.
  5. Monja Mohei
    Super popular Monja place, with multiple locations, I had to stop by to check it out...
  6. Ueno Park Monument
    Stopped by to admire the entrance of the park!
  7. Kuromon Ruins
    This used to be the main gate of the Kan-eiji temple.. the temple that occupied almost all of this park!
  8. The Last Samurai
    Saigo Takamori played a big role in the battles that led Japan away from a feudal society.
  9. Funeral Monument to the Shogi-tai
    The Shogi-tai was an army of the Edo Shogunate.
  10. Kiyomizu Temple
    I entered here from the East side... and existed through the west side.
  11. Moon Tree
    When I passed by Kiyomizu temple again from the west side, I saw this interesting tree...so I stopped to appreciate the temple again from this side.
  12. Bento-do Temple
    In the middle of Shinobazuno pond, you'll find this nice temple.
  13. Boat Dock
    If during your visit to Bento-do temple you have some time, you can go for a 30 minute boat ride here.
  14. Masaoka Shiki Monument
    He was a major figure in the development of modern Haiku Poetry!
  15. Hanazono Inari Shrine
    You may find this place looks somewhat familiar to a place you just visited recently...
  16. Stairs to Ueno Daibutsu
    Walking down the Sakura Spot path, you'll see a set of stairs with a ä½› sign on your left, go up from here!
  17. Ueno Daibutsu
    Remains of a bronze 17th century Buddha that was destroyed by an earthquake.
  18. Giant Lantern
    Many people call this the monster lantern.
  19. Suisha Gate
    There used to be a building here...then it was a gate..and even the gate now is quite damaged...
  20. Pagoda of Ken'ei-ji Temple
    If you look right after passing Suisha Gate, you'll see a nice pagoda!
  21. Stone Lanterns
    Walking towards the Toshogu Temple, there were many tourists that asked me to help them take pictures with the stone lanterns.
  22. Ueno Toshogu Shrine
    Luxurious temple....this was built in the 17th century. Take a look from a bit further away so you see all the fencing and accompanying structures.
  23. Uguisu Dango
    I stopped by here for a break and some dango, perhaps you should do the same.
  24. Big Fountain of Ueno Park
    This is a nice open area across from the Tokyo National Museum.
  25. Ueshima Coffee
    Take a look at this shop from Byobuzaka Street. See you soon!
