I was in Ueno the day I lost my mail bag. Below are the types of places I stopped by...

Vendors

My first stop of the day was Ameyoko. This street has around 80 years of history. The name ameyoko has a double meaning. Ameyoko stands for from "Ameya Yokocho" which means candy store alley, but Ame is also short for America. After WWII, Ameyoko was a black market for American goods which were banned. Anyway, I'm rambling a bit here...I'm thinking I could have been distracted while visiting one of the stores here..because there are so many! Once you find the stores that I've been to, please take a picture of the store front including the store sign!

Shrines and Temples

After answering to my appetite in Ameyoko, I walked to Ueno park. There are many beautiful temples and shrines in the park. I visited the Kiyomizu temple, the Bento-do temple, the Hanazono Inari shrine, and the Ueno Toshogu shrine... Perhaps I was forgetful and left my mail bag while going to the shrine to worship? When you arrive at these destinations, please take a picture of the exterior from where I passed by. The view may help me recall what I did while I was there.

Park Attractions

While walking around Ueno park I also stopped here and there to admire the miscellaneous landmarks, statues and memorials scattered around the park. Many of these have much history associated with them. I'm wondering if I might have got carried away with helping tourists take pictures. When you get to these attractions, please take a picture of the entirety of the landmark.

Here is a more detailed list of all the places I stopped by. Since I was making deliveries while wandering around, you may find there is an more efficient order to visit these places... Maybe ask my friend Mia for the optimal path to hit all these locations!

Please take a picture of each of these items.

  1. Ameyoko Shopping District
    I entered Ameyoko from Okachimachi Station. Try capturing the "Ameyoko" Sign at this entrance.
  2. Ameyoko Plaza
    There is a Tokyo Whiskey Store in this plaza, I must have entered from the entrance closest to that. Take a picture of the Plaza Sign at the entrance.
  3. Monja Mohei
    Super popular Monja place, with multiple locations. It was too busy so I just stopped briefly in front of the store.
  4. Uo Kusa
    A small standing bar with seafood from Tohoku, friendly prices too...had to stop for a bite...
  5. Nakata Shoten
    You know how I said Ameyoko was a black market after WWII? This is a fun shop with some WWII era items!
  6. Ueno Park Monument
    Stopped by to admire the entrance of the park!
  7. Kuromon Ruins
    This used to be the main gate of the Kan-eiji temple.. the temple that occupied almost all of this park!
  8. The Last Samurai
    Saigo Takamori played a big role in the battles that led Japan away from a feudal society. Lots of people want to take pictures with the statue.
  9. Funeral Monument to the Shogi-tai
    The Shogi-tai was an army of the Edo Shogunate... I stopped here for a bit to read the history plaque.
  10. Kiyomizu Temple
    I entered here from the East side... and existed through the west side.
  11. Big Fountain of Ueno Park
    This is a nice open area across from the Tokyo National Museum.
  12. Bento-do Temple
    In the middle of Shinobazuno pond, you'll find this nice temple.
  13. Pagoda of Ken'ei-ji Temple
    If you look right after passing Suisha Gate, you'll see a nice pagoda in the distance!
  14. Suisha Gate
    There used to be a building here...then it was a gate..and even the gate now is quite damaged...
  15. Boat Dock
    If during your visit to Bento-do temple you have some time, you can go for a 30 minute boat ride here. I only passed by the entrace with the clock on top.
  16. Masaoka Shiki Monument
    He was a major figure in the development of modern Haiku Poetry. Japanese literature fans will visit here.
  17. Hanazono Inari Shrine
    You may find this place looks somewhat familiar to a place you just visited recently...
  18. Stairs to Ueno Daibutsu

  19. Giant Lantern
    Many people call this the monster lantern... I think you will see why.
  20. Uguisu Dango
    I stopped by here for a break and some dango, maybe you'll want a quick break here too? Take a picture of the entrance passage way before you get to the store. It is a little hidden!
  21. Stone Lanterns
    Walking towards the Toshogu Temple, there were many tourists that asked me to help them take pictures with the stone lanterns.
  22. Ueno Daibutsu
    Here lies the remains of a 17th century bronze Buddha that was destroyed by an earthquake.
